Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions About Our Community

Who is behind Eyes of an Addict?

Founded by D. Bailey, a Certified Peer Recovery Support Specialist with over 5 years in recovery. D. Bailey combines lived experience with professional training to create authentic recovery resources and community support.

How can I join the community?

You can join our community by following us on social media platforms (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok) or by reaching out through our contact form. We welcome anyone who is in recovery or supporting someone in recovery.

Are your products affordable?

Yes! Everything is designed to be accessible to people in recovery. We believe that financial barriers should never prevent someone from accessing recovery resources and support.

What makes your approach different?

Our approach is authentically peer-led. We're not a corporate wellness program - we're real people with real recovery experience creating tools and community for others on the same journey.

Do you offer coaching or consulting?

Coaching and consulting services are coming soon! D. Bailey is developing affordable, peer-led coaching programs designed specifically for people in early recovery. Stay connected for updates.

What kind of digital products do you offer?

Our digital products include recovery guides, daily affirmation collections, goal-setting worksheets, milestone tracking tools, and other resources created specifically for people in recovery by someone with lived experience.

How do I know if this community is right for me?

If you're in recovery, supporting someone in recovery, or curious about peer-led recovery support, you're welcome here. Our community is built on mutual support, authenticity, and recovery pride.

Can I get involved beyond just purchasing products?

Absolutely! We're always looking for community members who want to share their recovery journey, contribute to resources, or help support others. Reach out through our contact form to learn about opportunities.

Still Have Questions?

We're here to help! Recovery can feel overwhelming, and we want to make sure you have all the information you need to feel confident about connecting with our community.
